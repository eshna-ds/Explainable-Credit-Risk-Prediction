Business Problem

Banks receive thousands of loan applications every day. Approving a loan for someone who cannot repay it can result in financial losses, while rejecting a trustworthy customer can reduce business opportunities. Machine learning models help banks predict whether a customer is likely to repay a loan, but many advanced models act as black boxes and do not explain their decisions. This lack of transparency reduces customer trust and can make it difficult to meet regulatory requirements.

Project Objective

The objective of this project is to build a machine learning model that predicts whether a customer is likely to repay a loan. In addition, the project uses SHAP (Shapley Additive explanations) to explain every prediction by identifying the factors that influenced the model's decision. This makes the system more transparent, understandable, and suitable for real-world banking applications.
